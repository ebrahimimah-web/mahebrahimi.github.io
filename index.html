I am a Ph.D. candidate in Finance at Brandeis University School of Business and Economics. My research focuses on innovation policy, public and private R&D, and their impact on innovation and entrepreneurial finance. In my job market paper, I analyze how program managers at the Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) allocate funding, providing new insights into the role of expertise and information in shaping innovation outcomes.

More broadly, I develop empirical methods to study trends in entrepreneurial finance, corporate finance, household finance, and investment decisions. My published work in The Journal of Investing explores how stock rating changes affect investor behavior, and my working papers examine R&D allocation, innovation spillovers, and household resilience to recessions.

I am proficient in Stata and familiar with R, MATLAB, and Python, with experience working with large databases (e.g., WRDS, Compustat, USPTO, USAspending, CRSP, Refinitiv, etc.). My industry background includes roles in pricing strategy and business analytics, where I built financial models and tools to support strategic decisions.
