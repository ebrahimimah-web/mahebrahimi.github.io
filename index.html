I am a Ph.D. candidate in Finance at Brandeis University School of Business and Economics. I am an empirical researcher trained in finance, micro and macroconomics theories including theoretical and empirical perspectives on entrepreneurship, corporate finance theories, venture capital and entrepreneurial finance, asset pricing, options and derivatives, financial econometrics, and causal inference.

Topics of my papers span from public R&D and innovation to investments and household finance. I am proficient in Stata, and familiar with R, MATLAB and Python.

I would love to connect about my work and ways to collaborate.
